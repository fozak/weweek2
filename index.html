Why Does Northeast Ohio Celebrate Global Entrepreneurship Week?

Global Entrepreneurship Week (GEW) is a yearly, week-long celebration where entrepreneurs and support organizations have the chance to come together locally and host events and other support initiatives on a global scale. GEW is an initiative of the Global Entrepreneurship Network (GEN), which operates a platform of projects and programs in 170 countries aimed at making it easier for anyone, anywhere to start and scale a business.

Northeast Ohio (NEO) is a burgeoning ecosystem for entrepreneurs and businesses of all shapes and sizes, yet our region still has a lot of room to benefit from global cooperation. GEWNEO is an annual opportunity to understand how to best put our entrepreneurial resources to work and connect to opportunities outside of the region.

NEO is celebrating GEW so that corporates and startups, banks and venture funds, and universities and foundations can unite because of their differences to unleash new ideas and form new relationships.

Currently, the institutions and organizations that provide resources, services, and capital to NEO entrepreneurs can’t help but operate under silos. Many of the things that entrepreneurs need to prosper aren’t accessible. Sometimes this is due to funding or poor networking, but more often than not, it’s just politics.

We’re annualizing GEWNEO to establish a channel for support groups around the world to tap into NEO entrepreneurship.
